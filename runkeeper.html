Runkeeper

16
Mai, 2014

Created by: Runkeeper, LLC

Version: 4.5.2

Size: 39.5 MB

Rating: 4+

Compatibility: IOS 6.0 or later
RunKeeper Pro ($9.99) is one of the most popular fitness downloads for the iPhone, and it’s easily the best running app available. By using the iPhone’s built-in GPS, RunKeeper Pro can track your pace, distance, time, and calories for each run. Read more: Best running apps for the iPhone Using GPS to track your runs Running apps are a great way to get the benefits of a GPS watch without spending hundreds of dollars. There are a number of apps that track your pace and distance, but RunKeeper Pro stands out from the pack. One of the best parts of the app is its simple interface. Once the app locks on to a satellite -- a process that takes less than a minute in most cases -- just tap “start activity” to get going. Before you do that, you can also customize your activity by piping in music from your iPod playlist or choosing one of three training workouts. If RunKeeper Pro has problems getting a strong GPS signal, you’ll get a message saying “accurate location unavailable.” You can still start your run and hope that the app acquires satellites eventually, but your distance information won’t be accurate. You can also try walking to a different location -- away from buildings or thick tree cover, for example -- and tap “try again.” The GPS icon in the upper right-hand corner will turn green if you have a good signal; red means it’s not connected. I usually don’t have any problems acquiring a signal, but it can vary from location to location. Other Features Once you start your run, RunKeeper Pro displays your distance and pace in large, easy-to-read numbers. Time, average time, and calories are also displayed. Swipe to the right and you’ll see your mile splits. The app also gives you customizable audio prompts that tell you time and distance every five minutes or every mile. I find the prompts very annoying, so I just choose to turn them off altogether. When your run is over, you can discard it or save it to your history. That’s another benefit to RunKeeper Pro -- it doubles as a running log, saving all of your data for each workout. The app displays each run in a list format, but you can sign up for a free account at RunKeeper.com to get cumulative totals and more analysis. You can also view any of your runs on a map. One big downside is that the iPhone isn’t as easy to run with as a GPS watch. You’ll want to invest in a quality armband (like the XtremeMac SportWrap) because otherwise the iPhone will drive you crazy as it bounces around on your arm (of course, this isn't an issue unique to RunKeeper Pro). The Bottom Line If you plan to run with your iPhone, RunKeeper Pro should be one of your first app downloads. I’ve found that RunKeeper Pro is just as accurate as other GPS workout trackers, and it’s easier to use than other running apps. The interface is pretty streamlined, which makes it easy to view your workout totals mid-run. The app may have issues getting a reliable satellite signal depending on your location (lots of buildings or trees in the way, etc.), but I haven’t had many problems in that regard. Overall rating: 4.5 stars out of 5. What You’ll Need RunKeeper Pro is compatible with GPS-enabled devices including the iPhone 3G, 3GS and 4.
